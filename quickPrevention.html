Quick Prevention

Your guide to understanding and preventing cyber crimes.

Phishing

Definition: Phishing is a cyber attack where criminals impersonate trustworthy organizations or individuals to steal sensitive data such as login credentials, credit card numbers, or other personal information.

How It Works

Attackers typically use email, social media, or messaging apps to send fraudulent messages that appear legitimate. These messages often include links to fake websites designed to capture your information.

Common Signs of a Phishing Attempt

  • Unexpected emails or messages asking for personal information.
  • Suspicious links or attachments.
  • Urgency or threats to create panic.
  • Generic greetings (e.g., “Dear Customer”) instead of using your name.

Prevention Tips

  • Verify the sender's email address and check for subtle misspellings.
  • Hover over links to inspect the URL before clicking.
  • Do not share personal or financial information via email.
  • Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) wherever possible.
  • Keep your software and security solutions up-to-date.

Ransomware

Definition: Ransomware is a type of malware that encrypts a victim's files, making them inaccessible, and demands a ransom payment in exchange for the decryption key.

How It Works

Once the malware infects a system, it typically encrypts important files and displays a ransom note with instructions on how to pay the ransom (often in cryptocurrencies) to regain access to the data.

Common Infection Vectors

  • Phishing emails with malicious attachments or links.
  • Exploiting vulnerabilities in outdated software.
  • Remote Desktop Protocol (RDP) attacks.

Prevention Tips

  • Regularly update your operating system and all installed software.
  • Backup your important files frequently and store backups offline or in a secure cloud service.
  • Educate yourself and your team on recognizing suspicious emails and websites.
  • Use reputable antivirus and anti-malware solutions.
  • Disable unnecessary remote access features and secure your network.

Additional Cybercrime Prevention Tips

Stay Informed: Cyber threats are constantly evolving. Regularly update yourself with the latest cybersecurity news and best practices.

Use Strong Passwords: Use complex passwords that combine letters, numbers, and symbols. Consider using a password manager.

Monitor Your Accounts: Keep an eye on your bank and online accounts for any suspicious activity.

Secure Your Network: Use firewalls and encryption to protect your data. Ensure your Wi-Fi network is secure with a strong password.
